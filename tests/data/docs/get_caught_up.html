## Style guide: Used in both before and during modes, the goal of the get caught up card is to provide a 7 to 10 item link roundup of the most important stories and things to know before and during an event. The items in the roundup should be tailored to one of three contexts: Before mode (between events), Before mode (day of event), and During mode (facts for tonight). Read the style guide for a description of each context. For live updates during the event, update the What’s Happening card instead.

  1. Ted Cruz hopes for a big win after a not-so-good week for Donald Trump. Cruz is expected to do well in the most populous southeastern areas of the state around Milwaukee and its surrounding areas.
  2. Bernie Sanders and Hillary Clinton have been campaigning hard in the state. Sanders hopes to cut into Clinton’s pledged-delegate lead.
  3. Wisconsin has almost always voted for a candidate already winning, but that could change this year.
  4. The state has 42 delegates up for grabs on the GOP side. They’re awarded in a dual system -- 15 to the statewide winner and then 24 split to the winners of each congressional district. Of the Democrats' 86 pledged delegates, 57 will be awarded proportionally by Congressional District while 19 will be given to the statewide victor.
  5. Who’s ahead going in? Trump and Clinton still have delegate leads overall. See detailed, state-by-state counts in our Delegate Tracker.
