## Style guide: The goal of the "what's happening" card is to provide a roundup of key events over the course of the evening and help communicate the current status of the event. The card should be complementary to the live broadcast and provide context for the live results card. The what's happening card only appears in during mode. Read the style guide to learn more.

  1. Bernie Sanders has won Michigan, the AP projects.
  2. “What tonight means is that the … people’s revolution, the political revolution is strong in every part of the country. And frankly we believe our strongest areas are yet to happen,” Bernie Sanders said tonight in Miami.
  3. Donald Trump has won Michigan and Mississippi, per AP projection. Clinton is projected to win Mississippi.
  4. “Many people have called it a movement … the people want to be involved,” Trump said in Jupiter, Fla. tonight.
  5. “Running for president shouldn’t be about delivering insults, it should be about delivering results,” Clinton said in Cleveland.
  6. More than six in 10 Democratic voters in Mississippi voters were black. Clinton won 89 percent of those voters, to Sanders' 11.
  7. In Michigan and Mississippi, around nine in 10 GOP voters say they are either "angry" at or "dissatisfied" with the government, per CNN. 
  8. Who’s voting: Michigan, Mississippi (both parties), Hawaii and Idaho (GOP only).
  9. When polls close: 8 p.m. ET: Mississippi; 9 p.m. ET: Michigan (Most polls at 8 p.m. ET, some counties are in CST); 11 p.m. ET: Idaho; 1 a.m. ET: Hawaii
  10. Combined, there are 316 delegates up for grabs, including contests in Michigan and Mississippi (on both sides), as well as Hawaii and Idaho for the GOP.
