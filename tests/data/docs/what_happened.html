  1. Ted Cruz is projected to win tonight’s contest, coming off a bad week for Donald Trump. The 42 GOP delegates up for grabs Tuesday are awarded in a dual winner-take-all system.
  2. On the Democratic side, Bernie Sanders is the projected winner, according to The Associated Press.
  3. "Tonight is a turning point. It is a rallying cry," Cruz told supporters at his victory party in Milwaukee. "It is a call from the hardworking men and women of Wisconsin to the people of America. We have a choice. A real choice."
  4. In a statement, Donald Trump said “Donald J. Trump withstood the onslaught of the establishment yet again.”
  5. Speaking in Wyoming after his win, Sanders emphasized how far the campaign has come and how much  “momentum” it has picked up. “This is a campaign of the people, by the people, and for the people,” he said.
  6. Amanda Renteria of the Clinton campaign told NPR “We've always known that Wisconsin was going to be an uphill battle. And we just know that it's not reflective of the Democratic base as a whole.”
